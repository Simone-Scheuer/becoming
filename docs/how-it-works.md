# How It Works

## The Problem

You know what you should do. You don't do it. Not because you're lazy — because every morning is a blank slate of decisions, and the decision is where things break down. You make a plan, and by Wednesday it feels irrelevant. You set goals and forget you set them.

Becoming fixes this by removing the decision.

## The Loop

Every day runs the same loop:

1. **Morning: the system prescribes.** It reads your goals, your task list, and what happened yesterday. It tells you what to do today — specific tasks, in order, connected to what you said you cared about. You can negotiate, but the default is: the system decides.

2. **Evening: you score.** What actually happened? The system updates your targets, marks tasks done or rolled, and flags anything that's been slipping.

3. **Weekly: the system reviews.** Every 7 days, it scans the pattern — which goals are on track, which collapsed, what keeps getting skipped. It adjusts the prescription for next week.

The loop is the product. Over time, the system accumulates enough data to see things you can't see from inside any single day: that you always drop exercise when a project gets exciting, that tasks sit for exactly 4 days before you either do them or give up, that your best days have variety.

## Why It Works (When Other Systems Don't)

**It prescribes, not asks.** Most systems ask "what would you like to do today?" Becoming says "here's what you should do today, based on what you said mattered." Removing the decision is the intervention.

**It remembers.** Every check-in is a file. A new conversation reads the files. Nothing gets lost between sessions.

**It counts.** "3/4 sessions this week" is a fact. "Am I doing enough?" is a spiral. The system gives you fractions, not feelings.

**It has a floor.** Every goal has a bad-day version. The 15-minute minimum when you can't face the full thing. The system still works on your worst day because the bar lowers without disappearing.

**It flags without guilt.** "This task has been sitting for 5 days" is a fact. The system states it. You decide what to do. No nagging, no shame, no passive-aggressive reminders.

**It protects what matters.** Some things die when you track them. Music, play, creative noodling. The system knows to leave them alone.

## The Files

Everything is plain text markdown. No database, no cloud service, no vendor lock-in.

- **`profile.md`** — who you are, how you work. Read every session.
- **`goals.md`** — what you're working toward, with specific weekly targets.
- **`docket.md`** — your task list. Tasks track how long they've been pending.
- **`weeks/`** — weekly plans. One file per week.
- **`checkins/`** — daily check-ins. One file per morning, one per evening. This is your behavioral data — over weeks and months, it becomes a record of who you actually are vs. who you say you are.

## The Philosophy

Most people don't lack intelligence or desire. They lack a system that converts intentions into actions reliably, across days and weeks, through good moods and bad ones, without requiring the discipline they don't have.

This system was designed from inside that problem, not from outside it.
